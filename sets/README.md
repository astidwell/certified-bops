# Drum & Bass

The Drum & Bass section contains deliberately sequenced listening journeys built with the pacing and shape of a DJ set.

This is not a bucket of DnB tracks. Each selection should contribute to an arc, and each transition should move the listener somewhere.

## Musical Direction

The collection can span:

- Liquid and soulful rollers
- Atmospheric and intelligent drum & bass
- Classic late-1990s through mid-2000s techstep
- Darkstep and early neurofunk
- Modern neurofunk when it strengthens the arc rather than merely increasing loudness

Classic artists and foundational tracks should generally take priority over newer releases unless a set has a different stated purpose.

## Sequencing Philosophy

Think like a DJ arranging a set:

- Establish a clear opening mood before increasing pressure.
- Change energy, darkness, and rhythmic density gradually.
- Use melodic or atmospheric tracks as bridges, not random detours.
- Avoid back-to-back tracks by the same lead artist when possible.
- Save major anthems and peak-pressure tracks for moments that earn them.
- Let tension breathe; relentless intensity becomes flat surprisingly quickly.
- End deliberately, whether with release, devastation, or a clean comedown.
