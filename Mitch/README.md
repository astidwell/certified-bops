# Mitch

The Mitch playlists are built for calm, warmth, comfort, and nervous-system-friendly listening.

They should work for reading, relaxing, winding down, quiet background listening, and sleep without demanding attention or becoming emotionally theatrical.

## Musical Direction

Prioritize:

- Ambient and gentle instrumental music
- Soft piano and modern classical
- Warm drones, subtle textures, and spacious arrangements
- Nature-adjacent atmosphere when it feels organic rather than gimmicky
- Slow, stable pacing with careful transitions

Avoid:

- Harsh transients or sudden volume changes
- Overly dramatic meditation music
- Tracks that become distracting or emotionally heavy
- Unsupported claims about miracle frequencies, healing, DNA repair, or instant nervous-system resets

## Sequencing Philosophy

The sequence should feel like the room gradually becoming quieter.

- Open with accessible, reassuring pieces.
- Reduce rhythmic activity and harmonic tension over time.
- Use long-form ambient tracks carefully so they deepen the atmosphere rather than stall it.
- Avoid abrupt changes in texture, loudness, or emotional tone.
- Let the final stretch support rest or sleep without requiring a hard ending.
